My name is Trinh Nguyen. I am currently an undergraduate sophomore at the University of California, Riverside. I am studying psychology. I plan to go to graduate school after undergraduate school focusing on counseling. My career goal is to become a high school counselor. I want to become the person I needed the most when I was in high school. I believe every kid has the potential to be successful with good guidance. My passion is to bring guidance and healing to people who have been through stressful times in their life, especially young children. I want to not only guide them in a better academic route but also be there for them with their problems or any problem that they need help with. I want to bring the importance of mental health to adolescents. I want them to know that they are being heard and their feelings are valid.

As the oldest daughter in a traditional Asian family, I have to take in all the responsibilities as a second mother of my siblings at a young age. I was able to juggle everything on my plates during my high school career. I was president of VSA, Vietnamese Student Association, at my high school. I was also very involved in tennis, and other clubs within and outside of high school. I take accountability and responsibility for my work. I learned how to balance my time and tasks. I gained leadership experiences throughout my high school career.

I have many experiences talking and working with people. Through my volunteering and working in customer service, I have good people skills. I can work very well with others. I am an active listener and communicator.
